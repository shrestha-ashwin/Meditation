What is meditation?

Meditation is the practice of being mindful, the ability to be fully engaged with what we are doing right now and to be in the present moment. It means getting aware of yours thoughts, and feeling and observing them without any judgement on your part.

Without your active judgement, your thoughts and feelings disappear and you are free from your mind. You are truly at peace.

Why meditate?

Reduce Stress

According to research, meditation reduces stress which in-turn reduces harmful effects of stress, and also reduces anxiety leading to a healthier well-being.

Increases presence

Meditation increases your attention span which helps you perform tasks better, and helps you identify and reverse destructive thinking and emotional patterns.

Improves Sleep

Meditation can shorten the time it takes to fall asleep and improve sleep quality. Studies have shown that people who meditate stay asleep longer.

Physical Well-Being

Meditation can improve physical health by reducing strain on the heart which is caused by high blood pressure. Studies show that meditation lowers blood pressure.

How to meditate?

Believe it, or not. Meditation is simple to perform.

The focus of meditation is to bring your attention back to the present moment. The simplest way is to focus on your breathing regardless of all the thoughts in your head, and that's it!

  1. Some tips:

  2. 1. Feel your breath deeply, to be connected to the present.

  3. 2. It is natural for thoughts to distract you, but focus back on your breathing.
